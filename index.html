CASE 01 · ESPORTS ECOSYSTEM · 2018–2023

Rainbow Six Siege LATAM

"3,000 followers. Zero tournaments. Zero regional identity. Five years later: 400,000 community members, sold-out arenas, and a multi-country esports circuit."

THE CHALLENGE

No regional presence

In 2018, R6 Siege had zero official competitive infrastructure in Latin America — no tournaments, no channels, no community ecosystem.

Building from zero

Create a complete esports ecosystem starting in Mexico, with limited budget and no existing regional playbook to follow.

Multi-market scale

Expand from single-country to full LATAM program: Argentina, Chile, Peru, Uruguay, and coordination with Brazil.

WHAT WAS BUILT

DIGITAL ECOSYSTEM

Launched and managed official R6 LATAM presence across 6 platforms: Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, Twitch, YouTube, and Reddit. Later expanded to Discord, TikTok, and Threads as the community matured.

MULTI-MARKET PROGRAM

Scaled from Mexico to full multi-market operation. Ran simultaneous leagues (MXR6 and SUR6) integrated with BR6 into one unified LATAM event covering 17+ countries.

CREATOR & INFLUENCER PROGRAM

Recruited and managed ~16 community casters. Executed campaigns with Werevertumorro, Alk4pone, and Chivas Deportivo Guadalajara. Coordinated 3 international press trips with ESPN and Azteca.

CONTENT STRATEGY

Original stats graphics, cultural moments content, player profiles, and recruitment campaigns. Managed official in-game content localization for Ubisoft across Spanish-speaking markets.

Franchise community intelligence at scale isn't just monitoring — it's knowing which signal matters out of thousands of daily posts across eight different games and five platforms.

This work required building a consistent methodology, maintaining it for the Spanish LATAM region across 26 months, coordinating with international teams, and delivering reports fast enough to be useful the morning after a major announcement.

The result: a cross-regional intelligence pipeline that gave stakeholders an honest, structured read on what the community was actually thinking — not what the official channels were saying.
